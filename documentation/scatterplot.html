Why have we used ScatterPlot?

1.Shows patterns in the data: A scatterplot allows you to see patterns in the data, such as whether there is a positive or negative relationship between two variables. This helps in understanding how the marks in different exams are related to each other.

2.Helps to identify outliers: Outliers are extreme values that are far away from the rest of the data. Scatterplots help to identify outliers, which can be useful in understanding the distribution of the data.

3.Shows the strength of the relationship: The scatterplot provides a visual representation of the strength of the relationship between the two variables. If the points on the scatterplot are tightly clustered, this indicates a strong relationship between the two variables.

4.Provides context: By plotting the data in a scatterplot, you can see the context in which the data was collected. This helps in understanding the factors that may have influenced the marks of students in different exams.
