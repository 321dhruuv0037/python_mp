Histogram

A Histogram is a variation of a bar chart in which data values are grouped together and put into different classes. This grouping enables you to see how frequently data in each class occur in the dataset.

Why have we used Histogram?

1.Shows the distribution of data: A histogram is a type of graph that shows the distribution of a dataset. It displays the range of values on the x-axis and the frequency or number of occurrences on the y-axis. This allows viewers to see how the data is distributed, including any patterns, clusters, or gaps.

2.Clear representation of data spread: Histograms are particularly useful for representing large datasets, as they provide a clear visual representation of how the data is spread out across the range of values. Viewers can easily see the frequency of marks falling into different intervals, which can reveal information about the distribution of marks.

3.Easy to read and interpret: Histograms are easy to read and interpret, even for viewers who may not be familiar with the data. The bars in the histogram are typically drawn in a uniform way, making it easy to compare the different frequencies of marks.

4.Useful for identifying outliers: Histograms can help identify any outliers or extreme values in the data. These can be represented as bars that are significantly taller or shorter than the others, indicating a deviation from the overall trend.
