Density maps help you identify locations with greater or fewer numbers of data points.

Why have we used Density Heatmap?

1.Provides a clear visual representation: A density heatmap uses colors to represent the density of students in different parts of the area, which provides a clear visual representation of the distribution of students.

2.Captures local variations: A density heatmap captures local variations in the distribution of students, which can be missed in other types of visualizations, such as scatter plots or bar charts.

3.Easily understandable: Density heatmaps are easy to understand and interpret, even for people who are not familiar with data visualization techniques. The colors used in the heatmap make it easy to see where the concentration of students is highest.

4.Useful for identifying patterns: A density heatmap is useful for identifying patterns in the distribution of students across an area, such as clusters of students in certain neighborhoods or areas.
